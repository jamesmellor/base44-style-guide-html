The course

About this course

This BA (Hons) Acting programme trains you to become a versatile and employable actor across theatre, screen, radio, and voiceover. You'll develop a wide range of practical skills while building a professional portfolio — real work, for real audiences — before you graduate.

Training is built around collaboration with industry partners including Ustinov Studios, The Rondo Theatre, and Theatre Royal Bath. Bath Spa Productions, our in-house professional company, creates genuine opportunities to perform in high-quality productions from your first year.

We take an entrepreneurial approach to actor training. Alongside performance skills, you'll develop the business acumen and creative independence to build a sustainable career in a competitive industry.

Teaching

How you'll learn

Teaching takes place through a rich mix of practical studio work and small-group teaching. In years one and two, you can expect around 18 contact hours per week, with increasing independent practice in your final year.

Practical acting classes

Regular studio sessions developing technique across voice, movement, and character work.

Seminars and workshops

Industry-led workshops with directors, writers, and casting professionals from partner organisations.

Rehearsals and productions

Public-facing productions via Bath Spa Productions and SparkFest, our student-led festival.

One-to-one tutorials

Regular individual feedback sessions with specialist staff to support your personal development.

Adobe Creative Campus

As one of only three Adobe Creative Campuses in the UK, all students have access to the full Adobe Creative Suite — invaluable for screen and media work.

After you graduate

Your career

Our graduates work across stage, screen, and voiceover sectors. Recent alumni credits include UK national tours, Netflix and BBC productions, award-winning short films, immersive theatre experiences, and West End pantomimes.

Many graduates go on to establish their own theatre companies, move into directing, writing, arts administration, or teaching. Ongoing graduate support is available through our dedicated Grad Support Unit.

Admissions

Entry requirements

We welcome applications from students with a range of qualifications and backgrounds. All applicants meeting our entry criteria will be invited to audition — there is no audition fee.

A Levels Grades BBB–BCC. No specific subjects required.
BTEC Extended Diploma DDM–DMM (Distinction Distinction Merit to Distinction Merit Merit)
T Levels Grade Merit
International Baccalaureate Minimum 32 points
Access to HE 60 credits, 45 at Level 3, Merit or higher
International students IELTS 6.0, minimum 5.5 in each element
Audition required. Shortlisted applicants are invited to audition as part of the selection process. We also accept Accreditation of Prior Learning (APL) for applicants with non-traditional qualifications — get in touch to discuss your experience.
