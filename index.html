Design Principles

Five rules for designing with personality

These principles ensure every page feels unmistakably Bath Spa — creative, confident, and built for a young audience that expects more.

01

Lead with Emotion, not Information

Students decide with feeling first. Hero moments should evoke belonging and excitement before presenting facts. Data and details sit below the fold.

✓ Do this

Use bold imagery, short punchy copy, and student stories as the entry point.

✗ Avoid this

Don't lead with league tables, stats, or paragraphs of institutional text.

02

Design for Scroll, not Search

17-year-olds are native scrollers. Content should flow vertically with visual rhythm — alternating between dense and spacious, text and image, dark and light.

✓ Do this

Create visual variety with section-level mood changes. Use staggered reveals.

✗ Avoid this

Don't create flat, uniform blocks. Avoid long pages that feel like one continuous tone.

03

Make it Feel Handmade

Bath Spa is a creative university — the site should feel crafted, not templated. Organic shapes, expressive type, and unexpected layout breaks signal creativity.

✓ Do this

Use organic blob shapes, rotated elements, overlapping layers, and mixed media.

✗ Avoid this

Don't rely on rigid grids and stock corporate UI. Avoid cookie-cutter card layouts.

04

Confidence Through Contrast

Bold colour contrasts and dramatic scale shifts create visual confidence. Large type next to small detail. Dark sections next to light ones. Full-bleed next to contained.

✓ Do this

Push scale extremes. Use 72px headings next to 14px body. Dark hero into white content.

✗ Avoid this

Don't play it safe with uniform sizes. Avoid "everything the same weight" layouts.

05

Every Page is a Canvas

No page should feel like a template was filled in. Each page tells a unique story with its own visual personality while staying within the system.

✓ Do this

Vary hero treatments per page. Let content shape the layout, not the other way around.

✗ Avoid this

Don't copy-paste the same hero/3-column/CTA structure on every page.

Usage rule

Use amber highlights on dark navy only. Never use amber text on white or light backgrounds — contrast is insufficient. Limit to 1–3 words per block; over-use diminishes impact.

About the course

Where creativity meets craft

Our Fine Art degree is built around the studio. From your first week you'll be making work, testing ideas, and developing a practice that's entirely your own. We believe in learning by doing.

You'll study painting, drawing, sculpture, installation, and digital media — with the freedom to specialise as your practice develops. Our tutors are practising artists who bring real-world experience into every session.

Two-Column Text

What you'll study

A curriculum built around your curiosity

In Year 1, you'll explore the fundamentals — material processes, critical thinking, and the history of contemporary art. You'll work alongside students from every discipline, sharing studios and ideas.

Regular crits, tutorials, and group sessions help you develop your voice early. You won't just make work — you'll learn to talk about it, defend it, and grow from feedback.

Years 2 and 3 bring increasing independence. By your final year, you'll be producing a substantial body of work for your degree show — Bath Spa's annual exhibition that attracts galleries and creative employers from across the UK.

Optional study abroad partnerships and live briefs with cultural organisations give you real-world experience before you graduate.

The degree show

Your work seen by the world

Every year Bath Spa's degree show brings together hundreds of graduating students for an exhibition across both campuses — open to the public, attended by galleries, agencies, and creative employers.

Many students receive commissions, job offers, and gallery representation before graduation day. It's not a finishing line — it's a launch pad.

Imagery & Media

Photography that tells stories

Our photography should feel like peeking into real life at Bath Spa — warm, creative, and bursting with personality. Bath itself is a character in every story.

✓ Authentic & Creative
✓ Bath is a Character

✓ Photography Do's

  • ● Candid, authentic moments — students creating, collaborating, laughing
  • ● Natural lighting, warm colour grading (golden, amber tones)
  • ● Diverse students in real settings — studios, campuses, Bath city
  • ● Environmental portraits that show personality and place
  • ● Movement and energy — hands making, bodies in motion

✗ Photography Don'ts

  • ● Staged stock photography with forced smiles
  • ● Overly corporate settings (boardrooms, generic classrooms)
  • ● Cool, desaturated colour grading that feels clinical
  • ● Isolated headshots with no environmental context
  • ● Images that could be literally any university
